Why you must do away with your tattoos so as to join KDF

 Posted on July 17, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  359 words  |  Enda Jantzen

- Joining the Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) is a highly privileged opportunity

- But many are missing the chance for just one thing. Read on

Tattoos are considered a form of beauty for women and expression of boldness for men especially here in Kenya.

They can be drawn anywhere in the body using both permanent or temporal ink and quite a number of tattoo studios offer the service in Kenya.

But it's now turning out tattoos are denying hundreds of young men opportunities to join the Kenya Defense Forces (KDF).

Reports indicate that most youngmen from various parts of the country have been turned away from the ongoing recruitment.

Two young men were on Monday, October 10 sent away from a recruitment centre in Kendu Bay, Homa Bay County for having tattoos on their hands.

The two had just joined a queue of hopefuls after having removed their shirts as is the requirement.

But the officer leading the recruitment noted their heavily visible tattoos and asked them to leave the centre telling them they have been unqualified because of the paintings.

"We have rejected them because in the army, we have no vacancies for people with tattoos," said an officer leading the exercise in Homa Bay.

He revealed that many other young men from around the country have been turned away for the same reasons.

The KDF says no recruit is allowed to possess tattoos as they signify a lack of discipline.

It turns out that if you want to join KDF, you must make the painful choice of having to remove your prized tattoos.

Joining the KDF is considered one of the most privileged opportunities in Kenya and people will go to length to join the forces.The recruitment excessive comes with very stringent conditions among them physical fitness.
