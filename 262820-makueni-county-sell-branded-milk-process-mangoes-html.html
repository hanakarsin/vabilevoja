Makueni becomes first county to sell branded milk, process mangoes

 Posted on February 9, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  497 words  |  Enda Jantzen

- Makueni County has joined the league of milk producers in the country

- This is after a milk processing plant called Kikima Dairy Plant was commisioned

- The county also commissioned a mango processing plant which is set to revolutionize the food processing industry in the country

Makueni County has scored a first after launching a milk and mango processing plants.

The county,recently joined the league of milk producers in the country with the commissioning of Kikima Dairy Plant.

All these were made possible by Makueni County Governor,Kivutha Kibwana who couldnt help but share the good news with Kenyans through social media.

Taking to Facebook on Thursday,January 11 2017,Kibwana expressed his excitement over the commissioning of the milk and mango processing plants,terming them a game changer.

''MAKUENI FRESH milk hits the Market.KIKIMA:[11/01/2018], Thursday.Makueni has now joined the league of milk producers with the commissioning of Kikima Dairy Plant.''Kibwana wrote in part.

TUKO.co.ke has learnt that the Kikima Dairy plant has the ability to process and pack 300 litres per hour and 6600 litres in a day.

The dairy plant is owned by Kikima Dairy Farmers Cooperative Society who have already made contacts with several local supermarkets which will stock their products dubbed 'MAKUENI FRESH milk'.

The Dairy plant will now ensure that Makueni county produces 18 Million litres of milk annually against a demand of 340 Million litres,which guarantee market for 'MAKUENI FRESH milk'.

Kibwana also used the opportunity to express his confidence in fruit processing in the county.The governor revealed how Makueni is set to produce 4,000 drums of mango puree anually by processing 2100 metric tonnes of raw mango from across the country.

''The Makueni Fruit Processing Plant engines have roared to life again, to process this season's mango harvest.Over 72 Metric tonnes of raw mango all from Makueni farmers had been delivered to the factory.By the end of the season, the factory is projected to process 2100 metric tonnes of raw mango from across the country, producing 4,000 drums of mango puree.''
