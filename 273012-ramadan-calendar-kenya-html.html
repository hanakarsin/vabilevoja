The history of Ramadan

Ramadan started with Muhammad, who made it a habit of spending lone-some time figuring out several questions that troubled him. For the record, the holy month is an ancient observance in the Muslim culture dating back to around 610 CE.

It is also remarkable that Muhammed met Archangel Gabriel in one of his solitude moments around the Hira cave found on the mountain Jabal an-Nour near Mecca.

According to the Muslim religion, Ramadan is the holiest month in the Lunar calendar because it allows individuals to reflect on their personal lives, spiritual growth, discipline, and physical nourishment. Therefore, Eid al-Fitr and Laylat al-Qadr are all incorporated in the observance of the holy month.

The revelation done by Archangel Gabriel took place as Muhammad was in one of his regular meditation sessions. In honour of Laylat al-Qadr, Muslims commit to fervent prayers in the last ten odd days before terminating the holy month.

Why does the date of Ramadan vary?

The Ramadan 2024 dates are based entirely on the lunar calendar, hence frequent changes on the Ramadan timetable. A lunar month has 29.5 days on average, while a solar month has about 30/31 days, except for February's 28/29 days. Therefore, it is common for Ramadan to occur eleven days earlier than the previous one.

It is also imperative to highlight that different countries use different calendars for the holy month, considering differences in the appearance of the new moon.

How long is Ramadan in 2024?

Ramadan in 2024 is expected to last for 29 or 30 days. The exact start and end date depend on the sighting of the crescent moon. It began on the evening of Sunday, March 10, 2024, and will end on Tuesday, April 9, 2024.
