CISA certification Kenya: training centres, study materials, requirements

 Posted on March 5, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1650 words  |  Enda Jantzen

Are you looking to expand your academic qualifications? There are several courses that you can pursue in Kenya, aside from the regular courses offered by universities and colleges. These include professional courses, as well as certifications by various organisations. One of the top courses that are available to pursue is the CISA certification.

CISA stands for Certified Information Systems Auditor. It is a professional designation that is granted by the ISACA (Information Systems Audit and Control Association) to professionals involved with information systems assurance, audit control, and security.

The designation was established in 1976, and it has since grown to become the most accepted certification for systems auditor, with membership from over 180 countries. If you are looking to undergo CISA certification, below is a look at all the information you need to know about the course.

What is a CISA Certification?

CISA certification refers to the process of being certified as a professional with proven IT skills with regards to the auditing, assurance, control, and security of information systems. The certification is given after undergoing the CISA course, sitting and passing the exam, and then applying for accreditation (after meeting the minimum certification requirements). The certification is to IT and information systems what professional courses such as CPA are to accounting. The certification is recognised globally.

Who can pursue the CISA course?

There is no limitation as to who can pursue the CISA course and sit for exams. However, there are specific requirements that you must meet, usually to do with experience, before being awarded certification. Therefore, CISA course is best suited for people working in the IT industry under various capacities such as:

  • IT and information systems auditors
  • IT and information system consultants
  • Information systems security professionals
  • Network security professionals

CISA course information

The CISA course syllabus is divided into five parts that are referred to as domains. These include:

  • The Process of Auditing Information Systems
  • Governance and Management of IT
  • Information Systems Acquisition, Development, and Implementation
  • Information Systems Operations, Maintenance, and Support
  • Protection of Information Assets

Before 2011, there were 6 domains in the CISA syllabus, with the 6th one being Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery. However, it was split up and incorporated into some of the other 5 domains; business continuity was integrated into Governance and Management of IT, and Disaster Recovery into Information Systems Operation, Maintenance, and Support.

Each domain encompasses:

1. The Process of Auditing Information Systems

The first domain covers how IT auditors should examine information systems in line with the set IT audit standards to assist an organisation in the protection and control of information systems. Below is a look at the subjects covered under this domain:

  • The auditing practices and techniques of information systems
  • Techniques used to gather information and preserve evidence
  • Risk assessment and control practices used in information systems auditing

2. Governance and Management of IT

The second domain covers how an information systems auditor can assure an organisation that proper leadership and organisational structure and practices are in place to support the organisation’s strategies and objectives. It covers various subjects such as:

  • The purpose of IT systems standards, policies, procedures, and plans for an organisation
  • The frameworks of IT governance
  • How to develop information systems standards, policies, procedures, and strategies
  • The effectiveness of IT governance structure
  • Information systems management and monitoring controls
  • IT organisational structure and personnel management
  • Strategies and policies for contracting information systems
  • IT systems Monitoring and assurance practices
  • Adequacy of information systems Quality Management System
  • Development and maintenance of business continuity plans
  • The processes for invoking business continuity plans

3. Information Systems Acquisition, Development, and Implementation

This domain deals with how an IT auditor should provide assurance that the practices for acquiring, developing, testing, and implementing information systems are in line with the organisation’s strategies and objectives. It covers various subjects such as:

  • Development of practices for acquiring, developing, implementing, maintaining, and retiring information systems
  • IT project management practices and controls
  • Conducting reviews of IT project management practices
  • Control practices for acquisition, development, and testing of information systems
  • Reviewing IT project plans
  • Post-implementation reviews of information systems

4. Information Systems Operations, Maintenance, and Support

The fourth domain deals with the processes for operating, maintaining, and supporting information systems in a manner that meets the requirements of an organisation’s strategies and objectives. Subjects covered under this domain include:

  • How to conduct periodic reviews of an organisation’s objectives regarding information systems
  • IT systems service level management
  • Third party management practices
  • The processes used in information systems maintenance
  • IT systems operations and end-user procedures
  • Data administration practices used to determine and optimise database integrity
  • IT systems incidents and problems management practices
  • Changing, configuring, and releasing management practices
  • IT systems capacity and performance monitoring tools and practices
  • Reviewing the adequacy of data backup and restore solutions
  • Disaster recovery plans in the event of an information systems disaster

5. Protection of Information Assets

The fifth domain deals with the security standards, policies, procedures, and controls used by IT auditors to guarantee the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets. It covers various subject items such as:

  • Evaluating information systems security standards, policies, and procedures
  • Designing, implementing, and monitoring of information systems controls
  • Designing, implementing, and monitoring data classification procedures and processes
  • Designing, implementing, and tracking information systems physical access and environmental controls
  • Procedures and methods of storing, retrieving, transporting, and disposing of information assets

CISA exams information

After finishing the CISA syllabus, you can sit for the CISA exam in the next available exam window after paying for a registration (exam) fee. The exam has a minimum score, which you must meet to pass.

How much does the CISA exam cost?

CISA exam fees currently stand at $450 for members and $635 for non-members – early online registration. Late online registration (but before the registration deadline) stands at $500 for members and $685 for non-members. The exam fees are payable to the examiner, ISACA.

Aside from the exam fees, you can expect to incur other additional costs while pursuing the course, which include:

  • Tuition fees – payable to the institution where you are to undertake the course. The price varies from one institution to the other.
  • Learning material costs – purchasing learning materials are optional, but you might want to for a better chance of passing the exams. The documents include the CISA review manual which currently goes for $135 and online access for CISA practice question database, which costs $225.

What is the passing score for a CISA exam?

CISA exam uses the 200-800-point scale, where the raw score of the exam is converted into the scaled (common) score. The maximum score attainable in the exam is 800, where all questions are answered correctly. The minimum score is 200, attained where the candidate only answered a small number of questions correctly. The passing mark required for the CISA exam is 450.

Requirements for CISA Certification

CISA certification in Kenya is offered to professionals who have sat for and passed the CISA exam. However, there are some requirements that you must meet before being awarded the certification. These include:

  • Attaining the minimum passing score (and above) for a CISA exam
  • A minimum of 5 years of experience in information systems auditing. However, up to three of the required experience years can be substituted by specific work experience (auditing or working in the IT and IT-related fields) and college courses and degrees.
  • If you have met these requirements, you can submit an application for certification and pay an application processing fee (currently at $50). The application must also be filed within 5 years of the CISA exam passing date for it to be valid.
  • After attaining the certification, additional requirements are needed to maintain it. These include:
  • Abiding by the Code of Professional Ethics
  • Complying with the information systems auditing standards
  • Continuing education – at least 120 hours of CPE every three years, with a 20-hours minimum per year.

Types of careers you can engage in after taking a CISA course

Several career opportunities are available for CISA certified individuals. The chances are even more when you have other academic and professional qualifications, in addition to CISA certification. Some of the jobs you can hope to land after getting your CISA designation include:

  • Internal auditor
  • Information systems analyst
  • Information systems consultant
  • Chief information officer
  • IT audit manager
  • IT project manager
  • Network operation security specialist
  • Cybersecurity specialist
  • IT consultant
  • IT security officer
  • Privacy officer
  • IT risk and assurance manager

Being CISA certified can also help you to land more roles or even a promotion in your current job, leading to an increase in your salary.

CISA certification offers you several benefits, from access to more exciting career opportunities to salary increments. The certification is also a testament to the fact that you have the technical skills required to evaluate the effectiveness and adequacy of information systems. So, what are you waiting for?
