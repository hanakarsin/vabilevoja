Murang'a: Woman appeals for prosthetic arms for her 3-year-old son born without palms and fingers

 Posted on May 9, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  699 words  |  Lourie Helzer

A 28-year-old woman from Kiharu, Murang'a county is appealing to well wishers to assist her son get prosthetic arms to enable him feed and carry out other tasks once he joins school.

Cyprian Weksa Makali, the third born child of Beverly Nasimiyu Makokha, was born with a rare condition where his hands end at the wrist joint, leaving him without palms and fingers.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, Nasimiyu who is a waiter at a bar in the village said she was shocked to learn that her son did not have normal arms at birth three and a half years ago.

“At first it was hard but I finally accepted him and I now treat him like my other two children. To me he is a gift from God and I'm a so protective of him from any harm or danger that may come his way," she said.

According to the mother of three, she was kicked from her husband’s home after her father-in-law orchestrated their separation.

He claimed he could not allow his son to marry a woman who had brought forth a child who suffered a permanent deformity that he termed a bad omen.

“My husband had accepted our son and was ready we live together as a husband and wife but his father was opposed to our marriage and when call for our separation persisted, my husband gave in," she said."I had no option but to return back to my parent’s home at Bukhoholo village within Malakisi in Bungoma county," she added.

She added that with no income to provide for her son who requires special attention and her two other children, she left her home for Nairobi to go and look for menial jobs.

“I started working at construction sites and I was later employed to wash clothes for Asian families before settling to work as a barmaid at a bar at Congo area in Kawangware," she said.

She would send the little earnings she got to her mother for her children's upkeep but when her mother fell sick, she was forced to bring the children to Nairobi.

Nasimiyu said her greatest worry is her son who is about to join school but he cannot do so as he is unable to hold a pen or a book unless he gets the prosthetic arms, which she can not afford.

“I have not made enquiries about the actual cost but I was told they are expensive and they have to be changed now and then as his arms will outgrow them as he ages," she said."This will stop when he becomes an adult according to what I am told. The arms will be of great help as they will aid him to do things on his own, tasks I always assist him execute," he added.

Although Makali has learnt how to hold a spoon and feed himself, he will do it more better if he had the arms and they will also assist him to do other things that she does on his behalf.

“For now, I leave her under the care of a shopkeeper at a shop also owned by my employer located next to the bar as it is unlawful for him to accompany me to the bar," added Nasimiyu.

The mother also disclosed how she has become a subject of ridicule and scorn from people who at times stare at her son and talk ill about her.

She further urged parents whose children have various forms of challenges not to hide them but instead bring them out.

By Mark Wachira, TUKO correspondent-Murang'a county
