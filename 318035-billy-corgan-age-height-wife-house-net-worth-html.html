Billy Corgan’s biography

William Patrick Corgan Jr. was born on 17th March 1967 at Columbus Hospital in Lincoln Park neighbourhood, Chicago. He was the eldest son of William Corgan Sr, rock/blues professional guitarist, and Martha Louise Maes Corgan Lutz. Corgan was raised in a Catholic background.

His parents also had one more son before they divorced in the year 1970. Billy Corgan’s brother, Ricky, went to live with his father as soon as he remarried a flight attendant. Billy also joined them in Glendale Heights, Illinois. He was allegedly physically and emotionally mistreated by his stepmother.

However, he developed a protective bond with his young paternal step-brother, who was physically challenged as a child. Soon his father separated with his stepmother, and the three were left to live alone with her. Both of Corgan’s parents lived an hour’s drive from their stepmom’s house.

Billy Corgan’s childhood was full of many notable activities. Growing much faster than his age mates, he was an extreme athlete during his elementary school education. Other than being a member of Marquardt School baseball team, he was also tasked with collecting baseball cards. Corgan loved listening to all the Chicago Cubs games live.

Billy Corgan’s music career

Corgan started performing in a string of bands while still in high school. He graduated from high school as an honour student. He was offered a grant and scholarship from various schools plus a tuition fee that his grandmother had left for him. However, he decided to pursue music over education.

He moved to Florida in 1985 to join The Marked band composed of drummer Ron Roesing among other members. Soon, the band dissolved, and he had to go back to live with his father. During this time, he performed in Deep Blue Bream band with Wayne Static.

While still in Chicago, he devised the next project, Smashing Pumpkins band. He met with guitarist James Iha, and the two started working together to recording demos. He also recruited assist Darcy Wretzky whom they had met in a local show. The trio began performing in local pubs and clubs using a drum machine for percussion. Later, they recruited drummer Jimmy Chamberlin.

Their first album, Gish, fared better than they expected. It featured heavy metal and psychedelic rock into its sound and beats. Follow up records such as Siamese Dream became multi-platinum hits. During the period the band became famous around the world. Their 1995’s Mellon Collie and the Infinite Sadness was even more successful. The album was nominated for the seventh Grammy Awards and would be eventually voted ten times platinum in the country.

His dream came true in 2015 where he was announced as the Senior Producer of Creative and Talent Development Initiative by Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (TNA). However, he later left the company due to delayed payments.

In 2017, he purchased the National Wrestling Alliance (NWA) company, including its rights name, championship belts, and trademarks. His ownership took effect on 1st October the same year.
