Samburu MCAs unanimously pass BBI as 12 counties now endorse bill

 Posted on June 6, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  425 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

- All the 26 Samburu ward reps voted for the BBI Bill on Monday, February 22

- Samburu became the 12th devolved unit and the fifth Rift Valley county to adopt the legislation

- So far only one county assembly, Baringo, has shot down the bill

Samburu has become the 12th county to pass the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) Bill after its Members of County Assembly (MCAs) adopted the legislation unanimously.

Reports indicate all the 26 Samburu ward reps voted for the bill joining four other Rift Valley counties that have so far passed the document.

Rift Valley's Kajiado, Trans Nzoia, Laikipia and West Pokot counties passed the BBI Bill a few days ago after their respective MCAs supported the legislation overwhelmingly.

So far only one county assembly, Baringo, has shot down the bill after a stormy session that nearly turned physical.

Support of 24 counties

The BBI proponents now need 12 more counties to adopt the document to achieve the required support of 24 counties for the draft to be forwarded to the Parliament for the next process.

The proposed amendment will be forwarded to the president for assent if a majority of members of the bi-cameral House approve it.

A referendum will be held if the Senate and National Assembly fail to pass the bill, or if the bill relates to a matter specified in Article 255 (1) of the constitution.

Under Article 255(1), some clauses of the constitution must be subjected to a referendum if they are to be changed.

As earlier reported, President Uhuru Kenyatta thanked Nairobi members of county assembly for passing the bill on Thursday, February 18.

Nairobi Majority Whip Peter Imwatok successfully rallied the ward representatives to pass the bill saying they stand to benefit greatly once the proposed amendments are passed into law.
