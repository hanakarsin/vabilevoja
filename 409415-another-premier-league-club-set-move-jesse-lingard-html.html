Another Premier League Club Set to Make Move on Jesse Lingard

 Posted on February 12, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  413 words  |  Almeda Bohannan

- Lingard has been a revelation since he signed for West Ham on loan from Manchester United

- His nine goals in 10 appearances for the Hammers has led to a number of Premier League sides expressing interest in him

- Aston Villa is the latest club to want to sign the 28-year-old

- Dean Smith is looking for wide men in the summer and Lingard has been tipped as the perfect fit for the side

Premier League outfit Aston Villa have joined the race to sign Jesse Lingard if Manchester United are willing to cash in on the England winger in the summer.

Lingard, 28, has reignited his career at West Ham since joining the Londoners on loan in January.

He has scored nine goals and three assists so far for the Hammers and has been integral in David Moyes’ side’s push for European football.

According to Football Insider, Lingard is regarded as the ideal ‘Villa Player’ and has been tipped to fit seamlessly in Dean Smith’s side.

Notably, the England international can play anywhere from a winger to an attacking midfielder to sometimes a false 9.

In West Ham’s latest game, a 3-2 defeat at Newcastle, Lingard was paired up front with Jarod Bowen.

Despite his versatility, Dean Smith is looking to acquire two attackers who can play out wide.

Aside from Lingard, another one of Aston Villa’s targets is Werder Bremen’s Milot Rashica.

Rashica, like Lingard, is quite versatile and can play on both flanks as well as down the middle.

After an impressive first 10 appearances, West Ham are also keen on taking up Lingard on a permanent deal but everything is dependent on Manchester United’s willingness to sell.

Interestingly enough, Lingard actually has some roots at West Ham.

Many know the winger came up the ranks at Manchester United but he recently also disclosed that his first ever goal was for West Ham during trials.

“When I was younger I went to West Ham on trial, when I was nine," he said during a recent edition of roomates quiz alongside Marcus Rashord. "I scored my first goal."
