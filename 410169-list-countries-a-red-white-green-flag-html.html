List of all 12 countries in existence with a red, white, and green flag

 Posted on May 15, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1578 words  |  Almeda Bohannan

A national flag is a representation and symbol of a given country. The colours may stand for the past struggles, hopes, and aspirations of the citizens. Countries with red, white, and green flags settled for these colours because they best represent their culture and core values, among other reasons.

A flag is a national symbol; that is why it is flown high during events when a person from a particular country wins or at half-mast in respect to when a public figure dies. These flags have psychological meaning that conceptually represent the nation’s core values and condense history and memories.

Countries in existence with a red, white, and green flag

Here is a complete list of green, white, and red flag with symbols. Remember, countries with red, white, and green flag all chose the colours for different reasons. For example, red may represent fire in one nation and bloodshed by freedom fighters in another.

1. Algeria

Algeria is one of the red, white, and green flag countries in Africa, located in the north. This nation with a Mediterranean coastline and a Saharan desert interior has its capital city as Algiers. Algeria is currently headed by President Abdelmadjid Tebboune.

Design

The national flag of Algeria consists of two equal vertical bars, green and white, charged in the centre with a red star and crescent representing the nation's major faith, Islam. It was adopted on 3rd July 1962.

Symbolism

The white represents peace, and green, the star, and the crescent symbolise the Islam nation. Red represents the blood of those killed fighting for independence.

2. Republic of Belarus

Belarus is a landlocked nation in Eastern Europe, bordered by Russia to the east and northeast, Poland to the west, Ukraine to the south, and Lithuania and Latvia to the northwest. Its capital city is Minsk.

Design

Belarus has a red-and-green flag with a white-and-red ornament pattern placed at the hoist (staff) end. The current design, having a thicker ornament pattern, was adopted on 10th February 2012.

Symbolism

Red represents the blood shed by people who fought for independence, and green represents Belarusian forests. The National Flag of the Republic of Belarus has white drawn-on red decorations; this design is used in traditional Belarusian costumes. The gold hammer and sickle in the upper corner flag represent workers and peasants.

3. Bulgaria

Bulgaria is a Balkan country characterized by a mountainous interior, Black Sea coastline, and rivers, among other terrains. Its capital city is Sofia.

Design

The flag of Bulgaria is made up of three equal-sized horizontal bands of white, green, and red. This red, white, and green horizontal flag was adopted in 1881.

Symbolism

The white on the flag symbolizes peace, love, and freedom, while the green represents Bulgaria's agricultural abundance. The colour red represents the blood poured during the struggle for independence.

4. Burundi

The Republic of Burundi is a landlocked nation in the Great Rift Valley where East Africa and the African Great Lakes region converge. Its capital city is Gitega.

Design

The flag of Burundi has a white saltire which divides it into alternating red and green areas, and the centre of the saltire merges into a white disk with three solid red six-pointed stars outlined in green. It was adopted in 1967.

Symbolism

The white colour of the saltire represents peace, green represents the nation's expectations for future prosperity, and red depicts the nation's pain during its freedom struggle. The three triangle stars represent Burundi's three ethnic groups: the Hutu, the Twa, and the Tutsi.

5. Hungary

Hungary is a landlocked country in Central Europe, bisected by the Danube River, with Budapest as its capital city. The current ruler is President Katalin Novák.

Design

Hungary's green, white, and red striped flag (a red, white, and green flag horizontal) was adopted on 23rd May 1957.

Symbolism

In the flag of Hungary, red represents strength and bloodshed while fighting for independence. White symbolises freedom and faithfulness, and the green represents freedom, pasturelands, and mountains.

6. Iran

The Islamic Republic of Iran (Persia) is a nation in Western Asia bordered to the north by the Caspian Sea and northwest by Armenia and Azerbaijan. Its capital city is Tehran, and the nation has over 82.91 million.

Design

What flag is red, white and green? Iran's flag has equal horizontal bands of green, white, and red flag with the symbol of Allah, the national emblem, in red centred on the white band.

The Takbir is written 11 times each in the Kufic script in white, at the bottom of the green and at the top of the red band. Iran adopted the current version of the flag on 29th July 1980.

Symbolism

Green represents the Islamic religion, white represents peace, and red means courage.

7. Italy

Italy is a European country with a long Mediterranean coastline. Its capital city is Rome. The country has more than 60.36 million people, and the president is Sergio Mattarella.

Design

The flag of Italy features three equally sized vertical pales of green, white, and red, with the green at the hoist side. The flag as it is was adopted in 1946 and came into force on 1s January 1948.

Symbolism

Green represents the green fields of the Italian landscape, white represents the snow Alps, and red represents blood splinted in the Italian wars of independence.

8. Lebanon

The Lebanese Republic is a nation in Western Asia, bordered by Israel to the south, Syria to the north and east, and Cyprus to the west across the Mediterranean Sea. Its capital city is Beirut, and the nation has a population of over 6.856 million people.

Design

The flag of Lebanon has a horizontal white stripe enveloped by two horizontal red stripes. The white stripe is twice the height of the red ones —a Spanish fess, and the green cedar at the centre, whose width is one-third of the width of the flag, touches each of the red stripes. It was adopted on 7th December 1943.

Symbolism

The two red stripes refer to the bloodshed when fighting for independence. White colour represents snow as a symbol of purity and peace, while green cedar represents the bible.

9. Madagascar

The Republic of Madagascar is an island country in the Indian Ocean, about 400 kilometres off the coast of East Africa. It is the world's second-largest island country, and its capital city is Antananarivo.

Design

The national flag of Madagascar has a horizontal red stripe over a green stripe, with a vertical white stripe at the hoist. It was adopted on 14th October 1958.

Symbolism

White stands for purity of heart, loyalty, peace and freedom. The colour red represents the Madagascans' sovereignty, strength, and patriotism. The green represents the country's significant amount of agriculture.

10. The Maldives

The Republic of Maldives is a small archipelagic state in South Asia situated in the Indian Ocean. It is located in the southwest of Sri Lanka and India, and its capital city is Malé.

Design

The flag of Maldives has a green rectangle with a red border field and a centre bearing a vertical white crescent. The closed side of the crescent is on the rising side of the flag. This national symbol was adopted on 25th July 1965.

Symbolism

The red rectangle represents bloodshed during the fight for independence, green rectangle in the centre symbolizes peace and prosperity. The white crescent moon symbolizes the Islamic faith of the state and authorities.

11. Oman

The Sultanate of Oman is a country in Western Asia, found on the southeastern coast of the Arabian Peninsula. It is the oldest independent state in the Arab world, and its capital city is Muscat.

Design

The national flag of Oman has three stripes with a red bar on the left with the national emblem of Oman, i.e., a white dagger and two swords. The current version was adopted in 1995.

Symbolism

White symbolizes peace and prosperity, red the battles fought against foreign invaders, and green the fertility of the land. The swords and daggers represent the traditional weapons of the Omani people.

12. Wales, UK constituent country

Wales is a nation in southwest Great Britain. England borders it to the east, the Irish Sea to the north and west. Its capital city is Cardiff.

Design

The flag of Wales has a red dragon passant on a green and white field. The current version was adopted in 1959.

The red dragon of Wales personifies the fearlessness of the Welsh nation. It depicts the struggle between the Welsh and the Saxons.
