Nairobi Family Appeals for Help to Clear Mother's KSh 4.2M Hospital Bill after Successful Brain Surg

 Posted on April 8, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  525 words  |  Enda Jantzen

- Noor Abdalla was admitted to the Agha Khan Hospital in August 2019 after complaining of headaches and visual failure

- A diagnosis revealed she had a brain tumour which was successfully removed through surgery at the same health facility

- However, she developed complications after the surgery and suffered a stroke to half of her body

- Doctors placed her under induced coma after suffering convulsions; she was later discharged for home care

- She incurred a total of KSh 7,686,074 in hospital bills, which the family was unable to clear and is now left with a balance of KSh 4.2 million

The family of Noor Abdalla Rizeg is appealing to well-wishers to help them raise KSh 4.2 million to offset a medical bill she incurred at the Agha Khan hospital after undergoing successful brain surgery.

Noor was admitted to the health facility in August 2019 after developing severe headaches and visual failure and a diagnosis later revealed she had a brain tumour.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, Noor's daughter, Salma, said although the tumour was successfully removed, her mother developed complications that saw her admitted for several months.

"The artery vessel became small, causing the right side of the brain to swell, which led to her suffering a stroke to half of her body," said Salma.

The doctors placed Noor in an induced coma after she once again suffered convulsions and they inserted feeding and breathing tubes to holes created through her throat and stomach.

According to Salma, her mother was admitted to the Intensive Care Unit (ICU) for two months before she was moved to the High Dependency Unit (HDU) and later on to the surgical ward.

Noor was later discharged on October 25, 2019, and incurred a total bill of KSh 7,686, 074.

"The National Health Insurance Fund (NHIF) paid KSh 500,000 while Heritage Insurance paid KSh 1,250,000. We were able to raise through fundraisings KSh 1,807,508," said Salma.

The family which resides in Kamulu area is now required to clear the remaining KSh 4.2 million and raise extra funds for their mother's medication, homecare, nurse and physiotherapy.

Although Noor can now eat, she is fully dependent on her children since she still can not talk or walk.

The family is now appealing to well-wishers to help them raise the remaining amount to clear the hospital bill and focus on taking care of their mother.
