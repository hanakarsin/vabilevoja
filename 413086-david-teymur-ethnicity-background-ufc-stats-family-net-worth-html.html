Kickboxing and Muay Thai career

The mixed martial artist started doing Muay Thai as a youth together with his elder brother, Daniel. Even though he began in 2004 and the age of 15, David and his brother Daniel grew up watching their elder brother Gabriel competing in kickboxing and Muay Thai.

David had an awe-inspiring career as an amateur player. In 2009, he won a bronze medal at the International Federation of Muay Thai Associations World Championship A-Class in Bangkok, Thailand. He also won the following:

  • Nordic Championship in Muay Thai
  • Swedish national Championship - twice in Muay Thai and once in kickboxing

He competed professionally where he recorded 39-1 with 11 wins by KO/TKO. In 2011, he won the King of the Ring Tournament in Oldenzaal, the Netherlands.

Mixed martial arts career

David and Daniel started training in MMA while still competing in kickboxing and Muay Thai. He made his MMA debut on 19th October 2013 at International Ring Fight Arena 5. However, he lost by decision to Mattias Rosalind.

He rebounded from the loss by having his next two fights in the same promotion. On 5th April 2014, he defeated Veselin Dukov by unanimous decision. On 22nd November 2014, he defeated Gaik Pogosyan by a first-round TKO due to knees.

On 4th April 2015, he fought Robin Tuomi at Trophy Mixed Martial Arts 6. He won the fight by TKO due to leg kicks and punches in the first round.

The Ultimate Fight

In August 2015, the player was selected for The Ultimate Fighter: Team McGregor vs Team Faber. He joined The Ultimate Fight house by defeating Thibault Gouti by unanimous decision.

He fought against Johnny Nunez during the elimination round, where he won by unanimous decision. In the quarter-finals, he fought Marcin Wrzosek and lost by majority decision.

Ultimate fighting championship

On 27th February 2016, he made his fighting championship debut against TUF teammate and fellow Swedish fighter Martin Svensson at the UFC Fight Night 84. He won the fight by TKO after he knocked Svensson down with an uppercut in the second round.

He had his next fight on 6th August 2016 against Jason Novelli at UFC Fight Night 92. He scored a knockdown from straight left hand in the first round. In the second round, he scored two knockdowns, one by the straight left and the second by knockout. He dropped Jason to the canvas by a right-left hook combination.

Lando Vannata vs David Teymur was his third fight with the promotion. The fight took place on 4th March 2017 in the co-main event at UFC 209. He came in as a 3-1 underdog but still won the fight by unanimous decision. The fight was crowned Fight of the Night honours.
