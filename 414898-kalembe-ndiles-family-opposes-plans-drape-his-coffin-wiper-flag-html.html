Former Kibwezi lawmaker Kalembe Ndile's family has opposed plans by a section of Wiper Members of County Assembly (MCAs) and politicians to drape his coffin with a Wiper flag.

The proposal had been made by some ward representatives who claimed the late humorous politician was a member of the Opposition outfit.

Dee Kivuva, a Wiper operative wanted the party to take charge of Ndile's burial arrangements to the chagrin of the funeral committee and his widow Magdalene.

Besides draping his casket with a Wiper cloth, the proposal also included hoisting the party's flag at the burial site.

"Planning to use the burial ceremony to advance political party interests without consulting the bereaved family and the church is retrogressive," Kivungi Musyoka, a member of the funeral committee was quoted by Daily Nation.

Over 50 family members and friends were locked out of Ndile's funeral service held at St Veronica Catholic Church in Syokimau on Tuesday, June 8, due to strict COVID-19 protocols set by the Ministry of Health.

Ndile, who died on Sunday, May 30, will be interred on Friday, June 11, at his rural home in Mbui Nzau, Makueni county.

His family appealed to members of the public to make contributions to give him a dignified send-off.

The funeral committee estimated the burial expenses at KSh 8 million including hospital and mortuary bills.

"The committee appeals to leaders and members of the public to make in-kind and cash contributions in order to give Ndile a dignified send-off," the statement read in part.

The vocal politician breathed his last on the morning of Sunday, May 30, while receiving treatment at Nairobi Hospital.

Confirming his demise, the former lawmaker's family said the 57-year-old succumbed after struggling with illness for a long time.

Earlier reports indicated the former Tourism and Wildlife assistant minister had been diagnosed with pancreatic cancer.
