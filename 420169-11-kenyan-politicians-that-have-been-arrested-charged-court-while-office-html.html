11 Kenyan Politicians Who Have Been Arrested, Charged in Court While Still in Office

In March 2020, victims of the infamous Kamata Kamata Friday arrests got a reprieve after a court stopped the act, arguing that the practice chipped away at the democratic gains that the country had made since the promulgation of the Constitution of Kenya 2010.

As the practice is popularly known, Kamata Kamata Fridays refer to Friday arrests of high profile public figures by the police.

The act is feared as those arrested face a weekend in a crowded prison cell amid public ridicule before being brought before a court on Monday.

The Friday arrests have previously been condemned across the country over the years.

In 2018, for instance, MPs strongly condemned police for arresting citizens on Fridays and Public holidays and tabled a motion before the House over the same issue.

TUKO.co.ke has sampled below some Kenyan politicians arrested and charged in court while in office.

1. Rigathi Gachagua

The lawmaker is the latest victim of Kamata Kamata Fridays.

On Friday, July 23, he was arrested when detectives attached to the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) stormed his Sagana home in Nyeri county.

The Tanga Tanga politician faced several charges relating to money laundering and other economic crimes in which he was accused of unlawfully receiving KSh 7 billion.

On Monday, July 26, he pleaded not guilty to all the charges against him when he appeared before the Milimani Anti-Corruption Law Courts.

2. Moses Kuria

In April 2021, Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria was arraigned and charged with flouting the government's COVID-19 guidelines.

A Kiambaa court ordered the controversial lawmaker to either pay KSh 75,000 in fine or serve eight months in prison.

The charges preferred against him included failure to wear a mask, breaking curfew hours, and unlawful assembly.

3. Junet Mohammed

The Suna East MP was arrested on the morning of Friday, July 21, 2017, in Homa Bay county over what was believed to be hate speech remarks and incitement to violence.

4. Johnson Muthama

In June 2016, former Machakos senator Johnson Muthama was arrested and arraigned in court.

5. John Waluke

In September 2020, Sirisia MP John Waluke got a sigh of relief after a Nairobi court granted him a KSh 10 million cash bail after spending three months in police custody.

Waluke was found guilty of defrauding National Cereals Board (NCPB) KSh 300 million alongside his co-accused Grace Wakhungu and was sentenced to 67 years in prison or a fine of KSh 707 million.

6. Didmus Barasa

Outspoken Kimilili MP Didmus Barasa was in August 2020 charged with fraudulently selling a KSh 450,000 mortgaged car.

The vocal Tanga Tanga allied lawmaker was accused of selling the Toyota Hiace car registered as KBX 734E without the consent of Uwezo Microfinance Bank, which was the mortgagee.

He was arraigned at Kajiado Law Courts on the morning of Tuesday, August 4, but he denied the charges and was released on a KSh 100,000 cash bail.

7. Nelson Koech

Belgut MP Nelson Koech was among the four MPs allied to Deputy President William Ruto, who was arrested in Bungoma during the hotly contested Kabuchai by-election.

8. Babu Owino

In January 2020, the outspoken Embakasi East MP was arraigned in court after he reportedly pulled the trigger on DJ Evolve.

Babu is said to have pulled the trigger on the former B Club's resident Dj on the morning of Friday, January 17, after a dispute ensued between them.

9. Mithika Linturi

On Tuesday, May 11, the High Court temporarily stopped the police from arresting and charging the Meru senator over claims he forged signatures to secure a KSh 530 million bank loan using properties belonging to his estranged wife, Marianne Kitany.

10. Oscar Sudi

In September 2020, the Kapseret lawmaker was set free by the High Court sitting in Nakuru on a cash bail of KSh 500,000 or a bond of KSh 1 million.

The charge sheet showed the politician would face five counts of hate speech, assault of a police officer, unlawful possession of firearms and ammunition, and resisting arrest.

11. Cleophas Malala

The Kakamega senator was in March 2021, released on a KSh 2.5 million bond with a similar surety.

The lawmaker and seven other suspects were arraigned in a Kakamega court on Wednesday, March 10, over several charges.

They were charged with five charges, namely robbery with violence, assault, felony, causing injury to Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) officer, and property destruction at Mumani Primary School, which was the polling station.
