Former Tahidi High actor Kibunja has spoken about his delight after a real estate company revealed plans to gift him a house.

The thespian sat down with YouTuber Hiram Kamuhunjia to speak about life before and after the famous high school TV show and him becoming a homeowner.

Life during Tahidi High

At first, Kibuja wondered why he got the character of a cleaner and not a student, being that he was the same age as many of the student characters on the show.

However, he realised that it was a blessing in disguise, being that he could not outgrow his character, hence spending more than a decade on the show.

He said he was able to make some good money on the show and even bought his first car.

Investments ruined by COVID-19

The thespian also disclosed that he had bought event speakers just before the pandemic, and having tried MC-ing, he found this a good buy.

However, when COVID-19 hit, everything went from bad to worse as his sound system were now useless, and he had to sell some of the speakers.

He also turned to soap detergent to make some cash and branded it, saying people accepted it very well.

"God gave me another idea about selling soap, detergent and I branded it as 'Wow' and people accepted it, even guys from car wash because they saw it as one of their own," he said.

Kibunja also thanked his landlord, saying he was patient with him, knowing that he was a good tenant and was only going through a difficult period.

The actor then got a call from a friend to work as an MC for a real estate firm handing over houses, and even though the pay was little, he did it, and it worked out.

Having performed perfectly, the firm allowed him to continue working with them, and they ended up making a promise to build him a house.

Kibunja becoming a homeowner

When he was informed about being gifted a house by the firm, Mizizi Homes, the thespian said he was filled with joy and was teary.

He said he did not believe it at first but knew that it is God's work.

Kibunja said having helped Omosh get a new home, it was just God's doing that he also happened to get his luck.

He also thanked the CEO of Mizizi for his gift, praying that God blesses him abundantly.

CEO of Mizizi Speaks Kibunja's home

Speaking to Hiram on the Kamuhunjia Show a few days back, George Mburu, CEO of Mizizi Homes, explained that the company is on a mission to build an off-plan house for Kibunja.

"Kibunja came to Mizizi as an MC for a company event and he did a good job as much as we didn't pay him a lot. But now we want to appreciate him and since we can only give what we have, and our job is building," he said.

According to him, that is not all, as he intends to make him go past owning one house and have several others as investments.

He added that he had gotten the motivation to start assisting people in the showbiz industry because the public expects a lot from them. Yet, they too experience financial constraints like anyone else.

Details of the size of the house and the number of bedrooms are yet to be revealed.
