Why Banditry, Insurgency May Not End In Nigeria Aliyu

 Posted on May 1, 2024  |  1 minutes  |  189 words  |  Enda Jantzen

A former Governor of Niger State, Babangida Aliyu, has harped on the importance of education in ending banditry and insurgency in the country.

Aliyu while speaking in Abuja at the 2021 Sir Ahmadu Bello Memorial Foundation 2021 scholars’ Mentorship Forum, said the lack of education in communities limits intelligence gathering to tackle security issues.

“If we don’t educate our population, Boko Haram, banditry will never end because there will be people willing to fight for their rights and they see that right in terms of the fighting because you are wearing a better cloth,” he said.

He noted that the foundation in its interest to educate more students in the north has granted scholarships to 800 students.

Meanwhile, the convener of the Concerned Nigerians movement, Deji Adeyanju, has alleged that President Muhammadu Buhari-led government is supporting terrorist groups causing security challenges in the country.

The human rights activist noted that bandit terrorists, Boko Haram insurgents and killer herdsmen are getting support and help from the government.

Adeyanju further stated that the criminals are not more powerful than the military, DSS, Police and other security agencies in the country.
